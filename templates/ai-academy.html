Introduction to AI

Learn how AI understands and processes text through tokenization

What is Tokenization?

Before AI can understand your text, it needs to break it down into smaller pieces called tokens. Think of tokens like LEGO blocks - AI builds understanding by combining these small pieces.

Tokens can be whole words, parts of words, or even single characters. The AI learned which tokens work best from reading millions of texts.

Module 02

Understanding Datasets

Explore how AI learns from data using the QuickDraw dataset

What is a Dataset?

A dataset is a collection of information that AI uses to learn. Just like you learn from examples in school, AI learns from millions of examples in datasets.

QuickDraw is a dataset of 50 million drawings made by people around the world. Google used it to teach AI to recognize doodles.

QuickDraw Explorer

Draw something and see similar drawings from the QuickDraw dataset.

Draw something and click "Recognize Drawing"

K-Means Clustering

AI groups similar data together using a technique called clustering. It's like sorting items into different categories based on similarity.

3
50

Click "Generate Points" to create random data, then "Run K-Means" to watch the algorithm group them.

How K-Means Works:

  1. Pick K random center points
  2. Assign each point to its nearest center
  3. Move centers to the middle of their groups
  4. Repeat until centers stop moving

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the art of giving AI clear, specific instructions to get the best results. Think of it like giving directions - the better your directions, the better the destination.

1

Be Specific

Instead of "write a story", try "write a 200-word mystery story set in a haunted library"

2

Set the Role

Tell AI who to be: "You are a friendly science teacher explaining photosynthesis"

3

Give Examples

Show AI what you want with examples of good outputs

4

Iterate

Refine your prompts based on the results you get

Understanding Git

Learn version control - how developers save and share their work

What is Git?

Git is like a time machine for your code. It saves snapshots of your work, lets you go back in time, and helps teams work together without overwriting each other's changes.

Commit

A snapshot of your code at a moment in time

Branch

A separate line of development

Merge

Combining changes from different branches

Revert

Going back to a previous version

Module 06

Building AI Agents

Learn the workflow for creating your own AI-powered applications

What is an AI Agent?

An AI agent is a program that can understand instructions, make decisions, and take actions to complete tasks. Think of it as a helpful assistant that can do things for you.

Examples of AI agents: Siri, Alexa, ChatGPT, and even game characters that make their own decisions.

The Agent Development Workflow

Follow these steps to build your own AI agent:

1

Define the Goal

What should your agent do? Be specific.

Example: "An agent that helps students with homework questions"
2

Design Inputs

What information does your agent need to receive?

Example: "The student's question, subject area, grade level"
3

Create the System Prompt

Write instructions that tell the AI how to behave.

Example: "You are a friendly tutor. Explain concepts simply. Ask follow-up questions to check understanding."
4

Add Tools (Optional)

What actions can your agent take?

5

Define Outputs

What should your agent return?

Example: "A clear explanation, step-by-step solution, practice problem"
6

Test & Iterate

Try your agent, find problems, and improve.
